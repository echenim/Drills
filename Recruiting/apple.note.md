## Infrastructure Software Engineer


Hiring-manager read

They will likely test whether you can build practical, boring, durable infrastructure. Not fancy architecture for its own sake.

Questions may sound like:

How do you design a distributed cache or queueing system?
How do you operate Kafka/Zookeeper/Redis/Cassandra at scale?
How do you do safe rollout and rollback?
How do you debug high-concurrency bugs?
How do you automate fleet operations?
How do you handle partial failure?
Fit for you

This is a strong fit.

Your PubMatic and StrataLinks background gives you Kafka, distributed pipelines, low-latency systems, and production ownership. Your LinkedIn traffic infra adds infrastructure credibility. Your Terraform/Ansible knowledge helps, even if you are not expert-level.

Your best positioning:

“I have built and operated backend infrastructure where concurrency, messaging, latency, observability, and safe rollout mattered in production.”


## Senior Network Software Engineer

Hiring-manager read

They will look for someone who can sit between software teams and network engineering teams.

They may test:

How would you model network topology?
How would you safely roll out routing changes?
How would you debug traffic loss across edge/backbone?
How would you build a source-of-truth system for network devices?
How do you handle config drift?
How do you make a network automation tool safe?
Fit for you

This is probably your best overall fit.

Your LinkedIn Traffic Infrastructure experience is directly relevant: routing behavior, proxy behavior, deterministic routing, rollout safety, and correctness in traffic paths.

Your PubMatic RTB work also helps because it shows low-latency high-volume backend systems. Your Go/Java/C++ background fits the engineering side. Your Ansible exposure helps.

Possible gap: you may need to strengthen BGP/MPLS/Netflow/SNMP vocabulary before interviews.

Your best positioning:

“I have built and operated software close to production traffic paths, where correctness, routing behavior, observability, and safe rollout were critical.”


## Software Engineer — Distributed Systems, Compute Platform
Hiring-manager read

They will value engineers who can reason deeply about failure modes, not just write service code.

They may ask:

Design a distributed build system.
How do you make computations deterministic?
How do you prevent duplicate work?
How do you handle worker failure?
How do you design caching safely?
How do you debug flaky distributed jobs?
How do you balance throughput, fairness, and reliability?
Fit for you

This is a strong fit, especially because your background repeatedly shows determinism under pressure.

Your StrataLinks platform is useful here: deterministic execution, idempotency, replay safety, low-latency pipelines, safety controls, and observability.

Your PubMatic bidder story also fits: distributed bidders unaware of each other, budget correctness, high-volume ingestion, Kafka/Flink telemetry, sub-100ms constraints.

Your best positioning:

“I have built distributed execution systems where determinism, idempotency, replay safety, latency, and operational visibility were core correctness requirements.”
